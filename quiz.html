Did you know that 50 million tonnes of electronic and electrical waste are produced every year? The next time something breaks, try fixing it first before chucking it out! There are hundreds of helpful videos on YouTube nowadays and it’s a great way to learn a new skill :) If you aren’t able to fix it, try thinking of a way to repurpose the item before tossing it into the garbage.

Going shopping and buying new clothes can be fun and exciting, but you might want to think twice the next time you want to buy a new t-shirt. An average Canadian throws out 81 pounds of textiles every year! A lot of those clothes could have been given a new home. Try checking out a local thrift store the next time you’re thinking of buying new clothes, you’ll be surprised about the variety of clothes that thrift stores carry.

An average of 4 gallons of water is wasted every time the tap is left on when you brush your teeth! Easily reduce your water waste by making sure you close the tap while brushing your teeth. We challenge you to waste as little water as possible in your morning routines, show us what parts of your morning routine you could adjust to save water!

Washing clothes in warm or hot water requires a lot of energy because the machine needs a lot of energy to heat up the water. Many people believe that washing clothes with warm water is better for removing stains, but cold water works just as well. We challenge you to start washing your laundry with cold water and see. It will also lower your electricity bill and save you money in the long run :)
Redeem Reward
Turning off the lights before leaving a room, even if you plan to return quickly, can help reduce your ecological footprint and bring down the cost of your electrical bill. We challenge you to start making it a habit to turn off the lights and appliances in a room whenever you leave the room. You’ll be surprised how many appliances you keep on without even using them!

The internet can be a great place with lots of resources for all kinds of purposes, from educational to entertainment. However, there are many environmental impacts of electricity. Fossil fuel based electricity emits greenhouse gases which leads to global warming. Limiting our electronic usage to only what is necessary can be beneficial toward our planet as well as for individual mental health. Going outside and being in nature improves our mood and overall wellbeing. Staring at a screen for too long is also harmful for our eyes and can often affect one’s vision. Try setting a time limit on your electronic devices and seeing how much you really need to use them in your daily lives!

It may be convenient to just chuck old batteries into the trash, but when the batteries end up at landfills, they begin to decay and leak. This causes pollution of both the water and air, ultimately harming the environment. We challenge you to start properly disposing of your batteries, by bringing them to a facility that will take them in :)

A 10 minute shower uses an average of 25 gallons of water. Showering can lead to high amounts of water wastage, especially if they are longer than 10 minutes but we know that it’s easy to lose track of time once you’re in there! We challenge you to create a shower playlist of your favorite songs that’s under 10 mins and use it the next time you’re showering to gauge how long you’ve been in the shower.

Taking public transit or carpooling has many benefits. For example, it significantly reduces gas emissions, as well as traffic. Carpooling with a friend is fun and a great way to socialize while taking public transit is a great option too because you get to relax.

We understand that buying non-organic meats can be cheaper and you get more bang for your buck. However, non-organic meat contributes to the rising levels of greenhouse gases which are leading to global warming. We challenge you to start replacing one meal a week with organically sourced meats!
Redeem Reward
It may seem like you’re producing a lot of garbage everyday without any intentions to. A likely cause of this is that you aren’t sorting your garbage properly! Many people find it more convenient to just throw all of their garbage into one place without sorting it. We challenge you to start sorting your garbage at home into the following categories: garbage, compost, bottle recycling, paper recycling, and glass containers.
